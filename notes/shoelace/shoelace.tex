The challenge is to give an elegant proof of this:
\url{https://twitter.com/JDHamkins/status/1340346873236905985}. \\

Use the fact that if $(x_0, y_0), \dots, (x_n, y_n)$ are the vertices of a polygon where $(x_0, y_0) = (x_n, y_n)$, then its area is:
\begin{align}
    A = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{i=0}^{n} \det\begin{pmatrix}
        x_i & x_{i+1} \\
        y_i & y_{i+1}
    \end{pmatrix}
\end{align}
This formula holds true regardless of the position of the vertices if we use signed area. \\

In our case $y_i = 0$ or $y_i = 1$ and we get a telescoping sum so the answer is $\frac{1}{2}(x_r - x_l)$ regardless of whether lines cross, if we use signed area.
